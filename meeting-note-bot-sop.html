What the bot produces

After processing, every attendee receives a personalised email to their Perigon email address, plus a message is posted to the Perigon Teams channel. Both contain a link to the branded meeting notes document saved in SharePoint.

At the same time, all named action points from the meeting are automatically added to the Perigon Weekly Action List in SharePoint â€” each action logged individually against the named owner.

The meeting notes document itself is a fully formatted Word document (.docx) stored in SharePoint, containing:

  • An Executive Summary (3â€“4 sentences covering key outcomes and decisions)
  • Meeting Notes by Theme (discussion grouped thematically, not chronologically)
  • Action Points (named owner and task for each action identified)
💡

Always review before sharing externally. The bot produces a strong first draft, but check names, action owners, and client terminology before forwarding outside Perigon.

Step-by-step guide

Step 1 â€” Start the Teams recording at the beginning of your meeting

The bot requires a Teams transcript. Recording must be started during the meeting â€” it cannot be added retrospectively.

  1. During a Teams meeting, click the three-dot menu (Â·Â·Â·) This is in the meeting toolbar at the top of the screen.
  2. Select "Record and transcribe" â†’ "Start recording" A banner will confirm the meeting is being recorded. All participants are automatically notified.
  3. Leave the recording running for the full meetingIf you stop and restart recording partway through, you may get a split transcript. Record the full session in one go where possible.
⚠️

Important: The meeting organiser must start the recording. If you are not the organiser, ask them to start it before the meeting begins.

Step 2 â€” Download the transcript after the meeting

Once the meeting ends, Teams processes the recording. The transcript is usually available within a few minutes.

  1. Open the Teams Calendar and find the meeting It will show a "View recap" button.
  2. Click "View recap" You will see a video playback on the left and a Content panel on the right.
  3. Click the "Transcript" tab in the right-hand Content panel.
  4. Click the "Download" dropdown and select "Download as .docx"Do not choose "Download as .vtt" â€” the bot requires the .docx format.
  5. Save the file somewhere easy to find (e.g. your Downloads folder)
ℹ️

If the Transcript tab is empty or says "No transcript available", see the Troubleshooting section below.

Step 3 â€” Upload the .docx to the SharePoint trigger folder

  1. Open SharePoint for the Business Management site
  2. Navigate to the "Internal Meeting Transcripts â€” DROP HERE" folder This is the folder that triggers the automation.
  3. Drag and drop (or upload) the .docx file into this folder You do not need to rename the file.
⚠️

Only .docx transcripts should be placed in this folder. Uploading other file types may cause the automation to fail or produce unexpected results.

💡

You can also access this folder via the direct link included in the email notifications sent to all attendees after every meeting.

Step 4 â€” Check Teams and your email for the output

  1. Wait approximately 5â€“7 minutes The automation runs automatically in the background.
  2. Check the Perigon Teams channel A message will be posted with the meeting summary and a link to the full notes.
  3. Check your Perigon email inbox Each attendee identified in the transcript receives a personalised email with a link to the meeting notes document.
  4. Review the notes before sharing externally The meeting notes are saved as an editable Word document in SharePoint. Click the link in the Teams message or email to open and edit.
  5. Check the action list has been updated Open the Perigon Weekly Action List to confirm all named actions have been logged correctly.
Section 03

Troubleshooting

Work through the relevant checks below before escalating to Sally.

No transcript appears in Teams after the meeting
Likely cause

Recording was not started, or transcription is disabled on your Teams account.

Steps to resolve
  • Check whether a recording notification appeared during the meeting. If not, recording was never started.
  • Wait at least 10â€“15 minutes â€” Teams can be slow to process longer calls.
  • Ensure your Teams account has transcription enabled. Contact IT if "Record and transcribe" is greyed out.
  • If transcription is unavailable in your region or plan, escalate to Sally.
The .docx file is downloaded but contains no transcript content
Likely cause

The transcript was too short or Teams failed to process the audio.

Steps to resolve
  • Open the .docx before uploading â€” if it's blank or only a few lines, transcription did not capture correctly.
  • If content shows in the Transcript tab but the .docx is blank, try downloading again.
  • Poor audio quality will reduce accuracy, but the bot will still attempt to process whatever text is present.
No Teams message or email received after uploading the .docx
Likely cause

File placed in the wrong SharePoint folder, Zapier is paused, or incorrect file type.

Steps to resolve
  • Confirm the file is in the "Internal Meeting Transcripts â€” DROP HERE" folder on the Business Management SharePoint site â€” not in a subfolder or a different library.
  • Confirm the file is .docx (not .vtt, .pdf, or .txt).
  • Wait a full 10 minutes â€” the Zapier trigger can occasionally be delayed.
  • Check your Outlook spam/junk folder.
  • If still no result, contact Sally and ask her to check Zapier for errors on the most recent run.
Your name is missing from the email recipients or Teams message
Likely cause

The AI could not identify your name in the transcript, or your name is not yet mapped to an email address in the Zap.

Steps to resolve
  • Check that you spoke during the meeting â€” only active speakers are identified as attendees. Observers who did not speak may not be picked up.
  • If your name is consistently missed, contact Sally â€” your name-to-email mapping may need to be added to Step 6 of the Zap.
Action points are missing from the Weekly Action List
Likely cause

The AI may not have identified a clearly named owner for the action, or the action was not explicitly discussed in the meeting.

Steps to resolve
  • Open the meeting notes document and check the Action Points section â€” the AI only logs actions it found in the notes.
  • If an action was discussed but not captured, add it manually to the Perigon Weekly Action List.
  • If actions are consistently missed, contact Sally â€” the AI prompt may need adjusting.
The meeting notes contain errors or are poorly structured
Likely cause

The AI produces a first draft â€” some inaccuracy is expected, especially with speaker attribution or technical terminology.

Steps to resolve
  • Use the link in the Teams message or email to open the Word document in SharePoint and make corrections.
  • If someone joined without a proper Teams display name, they may appear as "Guest" or an email address.
  • If a specific section is consistently wrong, report this to Sally so the AI prompt can be adjusted.
ℹ️

For issues not covered here, contact Sally Weatherly (Growth & Operations Lead). Include the meeting name, date, and a screenshot of any Zapier error if available.
