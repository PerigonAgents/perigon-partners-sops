Section 02

Step-by-step guide

Step 1 - Start the Teams recording at the beginning of your meeting

The bot requires a Teams transcript. Recording must be started during the meeting - it cannot be added retrospectively.

  1. During a Teams meeting, click the three-dot menu (···) This is in the meeting toolbar at the top of the screen.
  2. Select "Record and transcribe" → "Start recording" A banner will confirm the meeting is being recorded. All participants are automatically notified.
  3. Leave the recording running for the full meetingIf you stop and restart recording partway through, you may get a split transcript. Record the full session in one go where possible.
⚠️

Important: The meeting organiser must start the recording. If you are not the organiser, ask them to do this before the meeting begins.

Step 2 - Download the transcript after the meeting

Once the meeting ends, Teams processes the recording. The transcript is usually available within a few minutes.

  1. Open the Teams Calendar and find the meeting It will show a "View recap" button.
  2. Click "View recap" You will see a video playback on the left and a Content panel on the right.
  3. Click the "Transcript" tab in the right-hand Content panel.
  4. Click the "Download" dropdown and select "Download as .docx"Do not choose "Download as .vtt" - the bot requires the .docx format.
  5. Save the file somewhere easy to find (e.g. your Downloads folder)The file will be named something like "Emma __ Sally | Strategy Day Follow Up.docx"
ℹ️

If the Transcript tab is empty or says "No transcript available", see the Troubleshooting section below.

Step 3 - Upload the .docx to the Google Drive folder

  1. Open Google Drive for the agents​@perigonpartners.co.uk account
  2. Navigate to: My Drive → Meeting Notes → "Upload Meeting Transcripts HERE" This is the yellow-marked folder that triggers the automation.
  3. Drag and drop (or upload) the .docx file into this folder You do not need to rename the file.
⚠️

Only .docx transcripts should be placed in this folder. Uploading other file types may cause the automation to fail.

Step 4 - Check your email for the output

  1. Wait approximately 4 minutes The automation runs automatically.
  2. Check the agents​@perigonpartners.co.uk inbox Subject line: "[Meeting Name] (Meeting Notes)"
  3. Review the attached PDF Check the notes are accurate before sharing.
  4. Optionally edit in Google Docs before sending to attendees The email contains a link to the editable Google Doc.
Section 03

Troubleshooting

Work through the relevant checks before escalating to Sally.

No transcript appears in Teams after the meeting
Likely cause

Recording was not started, or transcription is disabled on your account.

Steps to resolve
  • Check whether a recording notification appeared during the meeting. If not, recording was never started.
  • Wait at least 10-15 minutes - Teams can be slow to process longer calls.
  • Ensure your Teams account has transcription enabled. Contact IT if "Record and transcribe" is greyed out.
  • If transcription is unavailable in your region or plan, escalate to Sally.
The .docx file is downloaded but contains no transcript content
Likely cause

The transcript was too short or Teams failed to process the audio.

Steps to resolve
  • Open the .docx before uploading - if it's blank or only a few lines, transcription did not capture correctly.
  • If content shows in the Transcript tab but the .docx is blank, try downloading again.
  • Poor audio quality will reduce accuracy but the bot will still attempt to process it.
No email received after uploading the .docx
Likely cause

File placed in wrong folder, Zapier is paused, or incorrect file type.

Steps to resolve
  • Confirm the file is in My Drive → Meeting Notes → "Upload Meeting Transcripts HERE" - not in a subfolder or the parent folder.
  • Confirm the file is .docx (not .vtt, .pdf, or .txt).
  • Wait a full 10 minutes - the Zapier trigger can occasionally be delayed.
  • Check the agents​@perigonpartners.co.uk spam/junk folder.
  • If still no result, contact Sally and ask her to check Zapier for errors on the most recent run.
The meeting notes contain errors or are poorly structured
Likely cause

The AI produces a first draft - some inaccuracy is expected, especially with speaker attribution.

Steps to resolve
  • Use the Google Doc link in the email to make corrections before sharing externally.
  • If someone joined without a proper Teams display name, they may appear as "Guest" or an email address.
  • If a specific section is consistently wrong, report this to Sally so the AI prompt can be adjusted.
There is already a file in the upload folder - can I add a new one?
Answer

Yes. Old files do not block new uploads. Each new .docx is processed independently. You do not need to delete old files, though keeping the folder tidy is good practice.

You don't have access to the agents​@perigonpartners.co.uk Google Drive or Microsoft inbox
Likely cause

Access has not yet been set up for your account.

Steps to resolve
  • Request access from Sally Weatherly or Emma Walford - they can grant you access to both the agents Google Drive and Microsoft inbox.
  • Once you have access, consider downloading the Google Drive Desktop app to your Windows laptop. This mounts the agents Drive as a folder in Windows Explorer, making it much easier to transfer transcript files into the correct folder without needing to open a browser.
"Item Not Found" error when dragging the .docx from Downloads into Google Drive in Explorer
Likely cause

Windows sometimes struggles to move files directly from the Downloads folder via drag-and-drop into a synced Google Drive folder.

Steps to resolve
  • Instead of dragging, right-click the file in Downloads and select "Copy"
  • Navigate to the Google Drive upload folder in Explorer
  • Right-click and select "Paste" - this reliably places the file in the correct location
  • Alternatively, upload the file directly via drive.google.com in a browser using the upload button
ℹ️

For issues not covered here, contact Sally Weatherly (Growth & Operations Lead). Include the meeting name, date, and a screenshot of any Zapier error.

Section 05

Zapier technical reference

This section is for anyone who needs to maintain, edit, or rebuild the automation. The Zap is named "Teams Meeting Note Summary Tool" in the Perigon workspace. It has 7 steps and runs fully automatically once a .docx is dropped into the trigger folder.

⚠️

Only Sally Weatherly (Growth & Operations Lead) should make changes to this Zap. Editing a live Zap incorrectly can break the automation for the whole team. If you think something needs changing, flag it to Sally rather than editing directly.

Trigger  ·  Google Drive  ·  polls every 2 minutes

The trigger that starts the entire automation. Zapier polls the specified folder every 2 minutes for new files. When a new .docx appears, it fires all subsequent steps.

5
Writes Minutes from Transcript and Perigon Knowledge Base
Action  ·  AI by Zapier  ·  GPT-5.2 (Perigon API)

The core AI step. A more capable model reads the full transcript and produces structured meeting minutes. It uses the Perigon Partners Knowledge Source to correctly identify staff members, their roles, and context about Perigon's clients and mission. The output becomes the body of the final meeting notes document.

ProviderOpenAI (Perigon paid API)
ModelGPT-5.2
Input: transcript textText content from Step 3
Knowledge sourcePerigon Partners - Knowledge Source (for AI by Zapier)
Return as array?No
Prompt
You are an expert executive assistant and strategy consultant, skilled at producing professional, structured, high-quality meeting minutes. Using the knowledge source, you also have a high level understanding of Perigon Partners, their staff (and each role within the company) and their work/mission

Produce structured meeting minutes using plain text headings, subheadings and bullets - not Markdown.

Please produce the following:

A short executive summary at the top with no more than five bullet points.

A structured meeting summary from the transcript (not a transcript summary) with clear thematic sections that group ideas together, rather than following the order of the transcript.

Include strategic insights and implications where relevant, but do not make anything up.

A clearly separated Named Action Points section, organised by person, including only actions that are owned by individuals. Do not invent any actions.

Requirements:
Keep the content concise but high-value.
Use clear plain text headings.
Eliminate repetition and filler conversation.
Surface decisions made, whether explicit or implicit.
Convert any vague statements into clear actions where appropriate.
Do not invent actions that were not discussed during the meeting.
Do not include action deadlines.
Use a professional but neutral tone throughout.
Adhere to UK spelling conventions.

Conclude the document with the statement:
"Prepared By Perigon Partners Meeting Note Agent in Zapier."

For action points:
Group them by the person's name.
Include only actions that are owned by individuals.
💡

If speaker names are consistently wrong, check whether attendees had proper display names set in Teams. If Perigon context is wrong, the Knowledge Source document may need updating - flag to Sally.
