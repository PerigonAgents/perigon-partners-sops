Section 01

Overview & How it works

The Perigon LinkedIn Post Generator automatically produces three LinkedIn posts for Emma — one per post type — triggered by any recorded Teams meeting that passes through the Meeting Note Bot. There is no separate content capture step and no manual input required. The same transcript that produces meeting notes also generates LinkedIn posts, with nothing additional needed from the team.

The two AI layers explained

The automation uses two AI steps in sequence. Running them separately produces significantly stronger output than asking a single step to jump from raw transcript to finished LinkedIn posts in one go.

StepWhat it doesWhy it matters
Step 3
Refocusing meeting notes for LinkedIn		 Reads the raw transcript and extracts the most strategically useful material across 5 sections: Client World, The Outside World, Team Brain, Inside Perigon, and Recurring Conversations. Sections 3 (Team Brain) and 5 (Recurring Conversations) are weighted most heavily — these contain the most distinct, human insights.

Works from focused, curated material. This is what produces specific, Emma-sounding posts rather than generic LinkedIn content.

What Emma receives

An HTML-formatted email sent from [email protected] directly to [email protected]. The email body contains three complete LinkedIn posts — one Thought Leadership, one Trust Building, one CTA — ready for Emma to review and personalise before posting.

💡

These are drafts, not finished posts. Emma should review each post, add any personal context or current detail, and write in her own voice before posting to LinkedIn. The automation produces a strong, specific first draft — it does not replace Emma's voice.

Section 02

Step-by-step guide

Step 1 — Record your Teams meeting

Follow the same recording process described in the Meeting Note Bot SOP. No additional preparation is needed for the LinkedIn Generator — it runs automatically from the same transcript.

💡

Meetings where the team discusses client challenges, market observations, or where someone says something that makes the room pause tend to produce the strongest LinkedIn content. The richer the discussion, the better the posts.

Step 2 — Upload the transcript to Google Drive

Follow the upload process described in the Meeting Note Bot SOP. Uploading the .docx transcript to the trigger folder (My Drive → Meeting Notes → Upload Meeting Transcripts HERE) triggers both pipelines simultaneously. No separate upload or action is needed for the LinkedIn Generator.

⚠️

The LinkedIn Generator depends on the same trigger as the Meeting Note Bot. If the Meeting Note Bot is not running, neither will the LinkedIn Generator. Check the Meeting Note Bot SOP troubleshooting section first if nothing is received.

Step 3 — Emma receives the posts by email

  1. Wait approximately 10 minutes The full pipeline — transcript processing, meeting notes, LinkedIn analysis, post generation — takes around 10 minutes end to end.
  2. Check [email protected] The three posts are sent directly to Emma's inbox from [email protected]. The meeting notes PDF arrives separately in the agents@ inbox as usual.If the email lands in spam, mark it as safe to prevent this on future runs.
  3. Read all three posts in full Each is formatted in HTML and covers one post type: Thought Leadership, Trust Building, and CTA.

Step 4 — Emma reviews, personalises, and posts

  1. Read each post and identify which feels most natural to post this week The AI anchors each post to a specific insight from the meeting — if one feels off, the source material from that meeting may have been thin in that area.
  2. Personalise the post Add current context, a personal observation, or a specific detail that only Emma would include. This is what makes the post feel like Emma rather than a generator.
  3. Post or schedule to LinkedIn Recommended cadence: three posts per week, one of each type. Monday (Thought Leadership) · Wednesday (Trust Building) · Friday (CTA).
ℹ️

The AI output should never be posted to LinkedIn without Emma's review and personalisation. The posts are strong first drafts — the finished post should always feel like it came from Emma.

Section 03

The three post types

The automation generates exactly one post per type on every run. Together across the week they build both reach and relationship with Emma's ideal client audience.

Post Type 1 Thought Leadership

Goal: Reach and awareness. Position Emma as the most relevant expert voice for her ICP. The reader should finish thinking "she understands my world."

Tone: Expert, opinionated, generous with insight. Never hedged or vague.

Examples: "I attended this event — here are my three takeaways"; "New regulation has just been introduced — here's what it means for a 200-person financial services firm"; "How to navigate [challenge] without losing strategic coherence."

CTA style: A genuine question Emma wants answered — specific to the post topic, not a generic "what do you think?"

Post Type 2 Trust Building

Goal: Deepen relationship with the existing audience. Make followers feel they know Emma and how Perigon thinks. The reader should finish thinking "I feel like I know her."

Tone: Personal, honest, specific. Vulnerable where appropriate. Never vague or motivational.

Examples: A client situation (anonymised) that shows how Perigon thinks; a moment of self-doubt or growth; an internal team win described with warmth and honesty.

CTA style: Soft — a reflective question or "I'd love to hear your experience." No commercial message in this post type.

Post Type 3 CTA

Goal: Move warm audience members toward a next step with Perigon — a tool, diagnostic, conversation, or service. The reader should finish thinking "that's actually useful — I might take her up on that."

Tone: Value-led. Never pushy. Lead with a genuine insight, end with a low-friction invitation.

Examples: "Try our new ESG compliance tool"; "Let me know how Perigon Partners can help you in this situation"; "Behind on AI strategy? We've built a short diagnostic — happy to share it."

CTA style: Direct but low friction. DM / tool / conversation. Never "let's chat" or "reach out to find out more."

Section 04

Troubleshooting

Check the Meeting Note Bot SOP first if the meeting notes did not arrive — the LinkedIn Generator runs on the same pipeline.

Emma received the meeting notes but not the LinkedIn posts
Likely cause

Steps 3, 4, or 5 of the LinkedIn Generator Zap errored after the Meeting Note Bot completed successfully.

Steps to resolve
  • Wait a full 15 minutes — Step 4 (post generation) can occasionally be slow.
  • Check [email protected] spam/junk folder — HTML-formatted emails occasionally trigger spam filters.
  • Ask Sally to check the Zapier run history for errors on Steps 3, 4, or 5 of the "Emma LinkedIn Idea Generator" Zap.
Neither the meeting notes nor the LinkedIn posts arrived
Likely cause

The trigger did not fire — likely a file placement or file type issue at Step 1.

Steps to resolve
  • Refer to the Meeting Note Bot SOP troubleshooting section and resolve that first. Both automations share the same trigger — if the trigger is broken, neither will run.
The posts arrived but don't sound like Emma — they're generic
Likely cause

The meeting contained limited strategic or client-facing discussion, leaving Step 3 with thin material to work from. Short or operational-only meetings will always produce weaker post ideas.

Steps to resolve
  • This is expected for certain meeting types — not every meeting will produce equally strong posts.
  • If substantive meetings are consistently producing generic output, the knowledge base files in Zapier may be out of date. Ask Sally to review the Emma Walford Voice Guide and Top Posts reference in the Step 4 knowledge sources.
The posts reference incorrect details about Perigon, a client, or a service
Likely cause

The Perigon Partners Knowledge Source document is out of date, or the meeting transcript contained an ambiguous reference the AI misread.

Steps to resolve
  • Check whether the incorrect detail relates to something in the Perigon Partners Knowledge Source — if so, flag to Sally to update it.
  • Do not include sensitive or full client names in meeting discussions that are being recorded — the AI should work from anonymised descriptions only.
Fewer than three posts were generated
Likely cause

The AI hit an output token limit, or the transcript lacked enough varied content for all three post types.

Steps to resolve
  • Read the email carefully — all three posts may be present but one may be significantly shorter than the others.
  • If a post type is consistently missing, Sally should check the output token limit setting in the Step 4 Zapier configuration.
ℹ️

For issues not covered here, contact Sally Weatherly (Growth & Operations Lead). Include the meeting name, date, and a description of what was and was not received.

I include the transcript of a recent Teams meeting within Perigon Partners, a business consultancy that helps Chairs and CEOs in the UK get aligned on the one strategy that matters—integrating growth, sustainability, and resilience into a plan the board trusts and the exec team can execute. SECTION 2: The Outside World - What market trends, regulatory updates, or news are pertinent that our Ideal Client Profile (ICP) should consider? - Were there any significant regulatory changes, policy developments, or industry shifts noted this week? - Is there relevant information about AI, technology, or sustainability that could impact our clients? - Did we encounter any notable articles, discussions, or events this week that provoked insights or reactions? - What key takeaways should a CEO of a financial, infrastructure, or services company consider? SECTION 3: Team Brain - What insights, opinions, or instinctive reactions emerged from the team this week concerning strategy and sustainability? - Was there a moment when someone made a statement that paused the conversation for reflection? - Did anyone express a strong opinion or a contrarian viewpoint this week? - Is there something the team is personally grappling with regarding strategy, sustainability, or the role of a CEO? - Were there any analogies, phrases, or frameworks shared that stood out as particularly insightful or useful? SECTION 4: Inside Perigon - What internal happenings are worth sharing in a genuine and appropriate manner? - Are there updates on the team, hiring, or significant business milestones? - Did we utilise or improve any tools, frameworks, or methodologies this week? - Was there a moment of vulnerability, challenge, or growth that could provide valuable context? - Is there any aspect of how Perigon operates that could enhance trust with a senior audience? SECTION 5: The Conversation We Keep Having - What recurring questions do clients ask that we consistently answer in the same manner? - Are there any misconceptions we regularly address? - Is there a concern our ICP should prioritise that they are currently overlooking? - Are there any issues they are overemphasising that we need to reframe? Additionally, are there any upcoming events, deadlines, or opportunities that we should connect content to? INSTRUCTIONS: - Do not fully respond to every question. - Aim to choose two or three comprehensive answers across the sections to produce strong, focused ideas for Emma's LinkedIn posts. - Sections that typically yield the best content are Section 3 (Team Brain) and Section 5 (The Conversation We Keep Having) — these sections contain the most distinct and human insights.
💡

If the Step 3 output is thin or too general, the meeting itself likely contained limited strategic discussion. This cannot be improved by changing the prompt — it reflects the quality of the source material.
